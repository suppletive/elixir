Curriculum Vitæ

Tran Truong
I received my PhD in Linguistics in April 2022 from the University of Chicago. I am broadly interested in syntax, morphology, and Distributed Morphology. My dissertation, supervised by Karlos Arregi, is a crosslinguistic, crossmodular, and cross-theoretic study of morphological contiguity, elsewhere called *ABA effects. Phenomena within the scope of my investigation include allomorphy, suppletion, syncretism, and dialectal/idiolectal morphosyntactic microvariation, especially as these occur within the domains of Japanese honorifics and Arrernte kintax.

For the 2021–22 academic year, I am a member of the Linguistics in Higher Education Committee, a Founding Member of the First-Generation Access & Equity Subcommittee, and a Lead Fellow at the Chicago Center for Teaching & Learning.
